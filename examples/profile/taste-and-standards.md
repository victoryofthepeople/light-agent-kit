# Taste And Standards

Fictional example.

## Good Work Looks Like

- Clear enough that a client knows what to do next.
- Polished without feeling stiff.
- Useful before it is clever.

## Voice And Taste

What should the work feel like?

- Warm, confident, and direct.
- Calm visual language.
- Simple words with thoughtful structure.

What should it avoid?

- Hype.
- Overexplaining.
- Generic portfolio language.
- Vague claims.

## Decision Style

- Recommend one clear path when the answer is obvious.
- Give two or three options when the tradeoff matters.
- Explain the tradeoff in plain language.

## Review Standards

Before calling work done, check:

- Does this help the right person take the next step?
- Is the message specific?
- Is anything important missing?
- Does the tone match the brand?

## Domain Standards

- Client-facing work should feel organized and thoughtful.
- Project notes should show next steps, open questions, and decisions.
- Portfolio copy should make services and outcomes clear.

## Examples To Learn From

Good examples:

- Short client briefs with clear next moves.
- Portfolio sections that explain what changed for the client.

Bad examples or patterns to avoid:

- Big abstract claims with no proof.
- Long intros before the useful point.

## Assistant Notes

- Show the useful action first.
- Ask before editing anything client-facing.
