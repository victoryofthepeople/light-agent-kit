# First Session Walkthrough

Fictional example.

This is what a real first session looks like after onboarding: a messy brain dump goes in, an organized workspace comes out, and the session ends with one finished thing. Same fictional user as [onboarding-sample.md](onboarding-sample.md): Maya, who runs a small design studio.

## The Brain Dump

Maya pastes this, unedited:

```text
ok so the chen rebrand is due friday and I still owe them the second logo
round, marco never sent the photos for the bakery site so that's blocked,
I want to raise my rates next quarter but I keep not thinking about it,
need to invoice studio joy for march STILL, there's that workshop idea
I keep having about teaching figma to small business owners, oh and I
told priya I'd send her the portfolio feedback like two weeks ago, also
my website bio is from 2023 and embarrassing
```

## What the Assistant Does

One clarifying question, then it works:

```text
Quick check before I sort this: is the Chen rebrand the most urgent thing
here, or is the Studio Joy invoice the one that hurts most if it slips?
```

```text
Chen is urgent, but honestly the invoice. It's been six weeks.
```

```text
Got it. Sorting now. Everything lands somewhere you can see.
```

## Where Everything Lands

The assistant writes the files and shows the result:

```text
tasks/active.md
- Invoice Studio Joy for March. (first, it hurts most)
- Send Chen second logo round, due Friday.
- Send Priya the portfolio feedback, two weeks overdue.

projects/bakery-site.md
- Status: blocked on photos from Marco. Follow-up drafted for your review.

ideas/inbox.md
- Workshop: teaching Figma to small business owners.
- Raise rates next quarter. (flagged to resurface when quarterly planning starts)

tasks/active.md, later section
- Update website bio. (real, but it can wait a week)
```

Seven scattered worries became four files with an order. Nothing got dropped, and nothing urgent is buried under something loud.

## The One Real Thing

The session ends with finished work, not a plan to work:

```text
Done: your week, in order.

Today: send the Studio Joy invoice. I drafted it from the March numbers
in your notes. It needs your one-line review and a send.

Tuesday and Wednesday: Chen second logo round. That clears Friday.

Thursday: Priya's feedback. I drafted an opening line so it stops
feeling like a confrontation.

Blocked: bakery site. Follow-up to Marco is drafted and waiting
for your yes.

Parked where you can find them: the rates decision and the workshop idea.
```

## A Correction Becomes a Lesson

Maya pushes back on one thing:

```text
The Marco follow-up reads too stiff. He's a friend, keep it casual.
```

The assistant fixes the draft, then saves the pattern:

```text
tasks/lessons.md
- Messages to friends and longtime collaborators stay casual.
  Reserve formal tone for new clients and money conversations.
```

Next session, the assistant already writes Marco messages the right way. That is the loop: corrections become rules, and the assistant improves because Maya used it.

## What Makes This Work

- The brain dump goes in raw. Cleaning it up is the assistant's job.
- One clarifying question, then action. An interview is for onboarding; sessions are for work.
- Every item lands in a file Maya can open and read.
- Urgency comes from the user's own words, not from list order.
- The session ends with something finished: a sendable invoice, a sequenced week, two drafts waiting for a yes.
- A correction turned into a durable lesson before the session closed.
